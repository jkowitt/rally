Weekly Planning System

The rhythm that builds momentum and consistency

Consistency beats intensity. This system creates a weekly rhythm that turns good intentions into completed work. Do it the same way, every week, and watch execution become automatic.

The Problem with Most Planning

Most people plan when they feel motivated. They create elaborate systems when energy is high, then abandon them when life gets busy. The result: inconsistent execution and guilt.

The solution isn't more willpower. It's a simple system you can follow even on your worst days.

The Weekly Planning System

This system has three parts:

  1. Weekly Review (30 min) - Close out the previous week
  2. Weekly Planning (30 min) - Set up the coming week
  3. Daily Check-in (5 min) - Stay on track each day

When to Do It

Schedule your Weekly Review and Planning for the same time every week. Most people prefer:

  • Friday afternoon - End the work week with closure
  • Sunday evening - Start Monday with clarity

Pick one and protect it like a client meeting.

Part 1: Weekly Review (30 minutes)

1 Review Completed Work (10 min)

Look at your calendar and task list from the past week. Ask:

  • What did I complete?
  • What moved forward even if not finished?
  • What wins should I acknowledge?

2 Review Incomplete Work (10 min)

Look at what didn't get done. Ask:

  • Why didn't this happen?
  • Does it still need to happen?
  • What would make it easier next time?

3 Capture Loose Ends (10 min)

Clear your head by writing down anything still floating around:

  • Promises you made
  • Ideas you had
  • Things you need to follow up on

Part 2: Weekly Planning (30 minutes)

1 Identify Your Top 3 Priorities (10 min)

What are the three most important outcomes for this week? Not tasks—outcomes. What needs to be true by Friday?

Rule: If you can't name your top 3, you don't have priorities.

2 Block Time for Priorities (10 min)

Look at your calendar and block specific time for each priority. If it's not scheduled, it won't happen.

  • Block 2-4 hour chunks for deep work
  • Protect mornings for important work
  • Leave buffer for the unexpected

3 List Supporting Tasks (10 min)

What smaller tasks support your priorities? What admin needs to happen? Keep this list short—10 items max.

The 3-3-3 Rule: Each week, aim for 3 priorities, 3 hours of deep work per priority, and 3 pages of notes or output. Simple metrics you can actually hit.

Part 3: Daily Check-in (5 minutes)

Each morning, take 5 minutes to answer:

  1. What is my ONE most important task today?
  2. What time will I work on it?
  3. What might get in the way?

Each evening, take 2 minutes to note:

  1. Did I complete my most important task?
  2. What did I learn today?
  3. What should tomorrow's most important task be?

Sunday 7:00 PM Weekly Review + Planning (60 min)
Monday-Friday AM Daily Check-in (5 min before starting work)
Monday-Friday PM Evening reflection (2 min before ending)

Making It Stick

  1. Start small - Even 15 minutes of planning beats none
  2. Same time, same place - Make it a ritual, not a decision
  3. Imperfect action - A messy plan executed beats a perfect plan ignored
  4. Review the system - After 4 weeks, adjust what isn't working
