Business Overview Framework

Define what your business is, who it serves, and how it makes money

Before you can improve your business, you need to see it clearly. This framework helps you document the essential elements of your business in one place. Complete clarity before you add complexity.

Why This Matters

Most business owners operate with a fuzzy picture of their own business. They know it in their heads but haven't written it down. This creates three problems:

  1. Decision paralysis - Without clarity on what you do, every opportunity looks attractive
  2. Scattered execution - Without knowing who you serve, you waste time on the wrong customers
  3. Unclear value - Without understanding how you make money, you underprice and overdeliver

The Framework

Your Business Overview answers five fundamental questions:

1. What Do We Do?

A clear, simple statement of the core service or product you provide. Not everything you could do—what you actually do that creates value.

2. Who Do We Serve?

Your ideal customer profile. Be specific: demographics, psychographics, behaviors, and situations that make someone a perfect fit.

3. What Problem Do We Solve?

The pain point, frustration, or desire that your customer has before they find you. Real problems, not features.

4. How Do We Make Money?

Your revenue model. How does money flow into your business? What do customers pay for, and how?

5. What Makes Us Different?

Your positioning. Why should someone choose you over alternatives? This isn't about being better—it's about being different.

How to Complete This Framework

Step 1: Start with Reality

Look at your last 10 customers or projects. Don't write what you want to do—write what you actually did. The patterns in your real work reveal your true business.

Step 2: Be Specific

Vague answers create vague businesses. "Small business owners" is too broad. "Solo consultants making $100K-$300K who want to productize their services" is specific enough to act on.

Step 3: Test for Clarity

Read your answers out loud. If a stranger couldn't understand exactly what you do and who you help, revise until they could.

Step 4: Keep It Short

Each answer should be 1-3 sentences. If you can't explain it briefly, you don't understand it well enough yet.

Pro Tip: Complete this framework before any major business decision. When you're clear on these five questions, most decisions become obvious.

Next Steps

  4. Use it as a filter for every opportunity that comes your way
